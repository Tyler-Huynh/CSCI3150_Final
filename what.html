What is Universal Design?

Universal Design is the ability to make most if not all features of your website or any other design accessible for people with disabilities to be able to use.

Some examples of accessibility that can be found in websites is the ability to add a screen-reader to your website so people who have trouble seeing can still get information from their favorite website without having much trouble. Many devices such as Window's laptops or MacBook's have the ability to read the information present on the screen to the user, granted that screen-reader technology is implemented.

Another example of accssibility is the ability to make the website have good design and amazing contrast. Websites that have good contrast allow for the text that is present on the screen to be easily read. Good design allows the website to be easily navigated through without causing too much trouble to the user.

Norman's Heuristics

Norman's heuristics include different usabiltiy heuristics that were developed to help designers in making their designs more accessible, the following are the 10 heurisitcs:

Visibility of System Status

Match between System and the Real World

User Control and Freedom

Consistency and Standards

Error Prevention

Recognition, Rather Than Recall

Flexibility and Efficiency of Use

Aesthetic and Minimalist Design

Recognize, Diagnose, and Recover from Errors

Help and Documentation

7 Principles of Universal Design

Ronald Mace developed the 7 Principles of Universal Design, they are listed below:

Equitable Use

Flexibility in Use

Simple and Intuitive Use

Perceptible Information

Tolerance for Error

Low Physical Effort

Size and Space for Approach and Use
