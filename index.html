Importance of Universal Design

    • History of Universal Design

    Accessibility is a topic that has been in discussion for years. With roots in the civil rights movement and the Civil Rights Act of 1964. This spawned numerous laws that forced corporations and places of power to make things accessible to everyone especially the internet.

    Universal Design on the internet includes screen-readers, easy and thumbable sites that all people can enjoy regardless of what disability one may have. There are many pioneers that helped define exactly what accessibility really is. Some of those people are Don Norman and Jakob Nielsen, who developed the 10 usability heuristics better known as Normans heuristics.

    Norman's Heuristics

    Norman's heuristics include different usabiltiy heuristics that were developed to help designers in making their designs more accessible, the following are the 10 heurisitcs:

    Visibility of System Status

    Match between System and the Real World

    User Control and Freedom

    Consistency and Standards

    Error Prevention

    Recognition, Rather Than Recall

    Flexibility and Efficiency of Use

    Aesthetic and Minimalist Design

    Recognize, Diagnose, and Recover from Errors

    Help and Documentation
